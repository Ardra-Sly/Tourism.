LADAKH

In the northern embrace of India, Ladakh unfolds as a breathtaking spectacle, a high-altitude desert boasting rugged, awe-inspiring vistas, a treasury of vibrant cultural heritage, and an array of unparalleled adventures waiting to be explored. Renowned as "Little Tibet," it echoes the timeless rhythms of the Tibetan Plateau, weaving a captivating narrative of cultural affinity and shared mystique, inviting travellers to embark on an unforgettable journey through its storied landscapes and soul-stirring wonders.

HARIDWAR

One of the holiest pilgrimages in India, Haridwar, or the ‘gateway to gods’, is located where Ganga, the sacredest of all Indian rivers, enters the Indo-Gangetic plains. Located at the foothills of the Himalayas, Haridwar is a city of temples and ashrams and its pious ambience envelops everyone. Haridwar is one of the four holy Indian cities that host the Kumbh Mela, a pious gathering of millions of Hindu devotees every 12 years. The Ardh Kumbh is organised here every six years. It also hosts the Kanwar mela every year during the rainy season.

MANIPUR

Manipur Located in the North-East, Manipur is a state in India which is also known as ‘The Land of Jewels’. The state is surrounded by nine hills with an oval-shaped valley at the centre, the reason why the state earned the title. With a wide range of breath-taking locations, Manipur is a mini paradise for nature lovers as one gets to explore lush green surroundings. Apart from that, the state has a rich cultural past and is also a land of festivities.
